# NotesSamurai — Style Guide

> Internal studio handbook. This document defines the creative identity of NotesSamurai — the *why* and *what it feels like*. For technical AI-generation rules, see `Master.md`.

---

# Brand Philosophy

NotesSamurai exists for the person walking home alone under a streetlight, still deciding what their life is for.

It represents the quiet in-between moments of modern life — the commute, the rooftop pause, the 5 a.m. train — where growth actually happens. Not in grand victories, but in small, deliberate steps forward.

NotesSamurai is built on:

- **Hope** — not naive optimism, but the stubborn kind that survives tired days.
- **Growth** — slow, uneven, real. Never instant, never magical.
- **Melancholy** — a gentle sadness that isn't sad. Nostalgia and longing as fuel, not weight.
- **Urban life** — the city as companion, not obstacle. Concrete, rain, and neon holding space for a person's inner world.
- **Finding purpose** — the ongoing search, not the arrival at an answer.
- **Quiet determination** — resolve that doesn't announce itself.
- **The beauty of moving forward** — even without knowing the destination.

NotesSamurai is for anyone still figuring it out, one train ride at a time.

---

# Creative Mission

Every song exists to give a feeling a place to live — a moment someone recognizes but couldn't name until they heard it.

Every visual exists to give that feeling a body — a place, a light, a silhouette that says "this is what it looked like."

Music and illustration are not separate outputs; they are one work told through two senses. The music carries the emotional current. The visual carries the emotional setting. Neither should explain the other — they should simply breathe in the same rhythm, so the listener feels the song before they understand it, and sees the world before they're told what it means.

---

# Core Emotion

Every NotesSamurai project must move through this emotional current:

```
Loneliness → Reflection → Hope → Momentum → Transformation
```

These emotions may appear in any order or weight depending on the piece, but the destination is always forward motion.

**Never despair.** NotesSamurai can sit inside sadness, stillness, or uncertainty — but it never ends there, and it never presents hopelessness as the final truth.

---

# Visual Identity

- Anime cinematic rendering — feature-film quality, emotionally detailed.
- Modern Japan as the fixed setting — real, present-day, lived-in.
- Urban architecture as emotional geometry — stations, overpasses, apartment blocks.
- Rain as reflection and release.
- Neon as quiet company in solitude.
- Blue hour as introspection.
- Golden sunrise as renewal.
- Soft cinematic lighting — natural, motivated, never harsh.
- Minimal compositions — one subject, one feeling, one focus.
- Elegant framing — deliberate negative space, considered balance.
- **No visual clutter.** If an element doesn't serve the emotion, it doesn't belong in the frame.

---

# Storytelling Rules

Every Visual Story follows this arc:

```
Beginning → Conflict → Decision → Transformation → Hope
```

- **Beginning** — the ordinary moment, the character as they are.
- **Conflict** — an internal weight: doubt, exhaustion, loneliness, uncertainty.
- **Decision** — a small, quiet choice to keep going.
- **Transformation** — a shift, however subtle, in how the character carries themselves.
- **Hope** — an open door, not a finish line.

The ending is **not necessarily victory.** It is the sense that moving forward was worth it.

---

# Character Guidelines

- One default protagonist, consistent in every project.
- Appearance stays locked — same hair, same silhouette, same signature accessory.
- No unnecessary redesigns between songs, seasons, or releases.
- **The character grows emotionally, not visually.** Their posture, gaze, and presence in the frame evolve — their design does not.

# Camera Philosophy

Slow. Patient. Cinematic. Natural.

The camera never rushes a feeling. It observes rather than performs. Avoid fast editing, quick cuts, or attention-grabbing movement — let every illustration hold its breath long enough for the audience to feel it, not just see it.

---

# Editing Philosophy

Premiere Pro first — every visual is built to be finished in the edit, not in the illustration.

- Simple transitions only.
- Cross dissolve as the default connective tissue between scenes.
- Slow zoom to deepen presence.
- Parallax to add quiet depth without noise.
- **Motion only supports emotion.** If a movement doesn't deepen the feeling, cut it.
- **Never distract from the music.** The edit serves the song; the song is never decoration for the edit.

---

# Typography

Minimal. Elegant. Modern.

Typography should feel like a quiet signature, not a design statement. No excessive animation, no decorative flourishes — text supports the mood without competing for attention.

# AI Generation Rules

- Maintain consistency above all — every asset must feel like it belongs to the same world.
- Always reference `Master.md` for technical generation rules (character lock, environment rules, prompt structure, negative prompts).
- **Never regenerate an entire project because of a small mistake** — isolate and fix the specific element instead.
- Reuse assets whenever possible; consistency is a feature, not a limitation.
