**Wet clothing behavior:** Clings slightly to the frame; absorbs light rather than reflecting it.  
**Never change rules:** The outfit composition (jacket, stripe, hoodie, pants, shoes, watch) must appear in every scene without alteration.

## Expression Guide

**Emotion Scale (0–10)**

**0-2: Empty**
* **Facial expression:** Blank, disconnected.
* **Eyes:** Unfocused, looking past the environment.
* **Mouth:** Neutral line, closed.
* **Eyebrows:** Relaxed, flat.
* **Body tension:** Loose, almost slumping.

**3-4: Lonely**
* **Facial expression:** Closed off, guarded.
* **Eyes:** Downcast or averted.
* **Mouth:** Slight tightening at the corners.
* **Eyebrows:** Slightly drawn together.
* **Body tension:** Shoulders raised slightly, defensive.

**5-6: Reflective**
* **Facial expression:** Calm, processing.
* **Eyes:** Focused on a specific detail (reflection, horizon).
* **Mouth:** Relaxed, breathing evenly.
* **Eyebrows:** Neutral but attentive.
* **Body tension:** Grounded, still.

**7-8: Hopeful**
* **Facial expression:** Softening of features.
* **Eyes:** Catching the light, widened slightly.
* **Mouth:** Parted slightly or forming a barely perceptible smile.
* **Eyebrows:** Lifted gently.
* **Body tension:** Leaning forward slightly, open chest.

**9-10: Determined / Transformed**
* **Facial expression:** Quiet confidence.
* **Eyes:** Sharp, locked on the path ahead.
* **Mouth:** Firm, resolved.
* **Eyebrows:** Set, strong baseline.
* **Body tension:** Upright, perfectly balanced, ready to move.

## Pose Rules

**Standing:** Weight distributed evenly or resting on one leg; hands relaxed at sides or in pockets.  
**Walking:** Natural stride, eyes forward, shoulders moving in rhythm.  
**Running:** Controlled, athletic extension; focus remains on the destination, not the exertion.  
**Looking upward:** Chin tilted naturally, neck exposed, eyes tracking light.  
**Looking toward horizon:** Profile or 3/4 turn, rigid back, fully engaged with the distance.  
**Hands:** Kept natural; no clenched fists unless explicitly required; no exaggerated anime gestures.  
**Shoulders:** Used to convey weight; drop them for relief, raise them for tension.  
**Neck:** Keep anatomy realistic during head turns.  
**Balance:** Must always look physically grounded in the environment.  
**Things to avoid:** Action-hero poses, dramatic mid-air leaps, comedic reactions, extreme foreshortening.

## Lighting Interaction

**Night:** Deep shadows mask details. Rim lighting isolates the silhouette. Minimal ambient fill.  
**Blue Hour:** Low contrast. Skin tones appear cooler. Fabric blends into the environment.  
**Purple:** Soft gradients. Highlights begin to emerge on the hair and shoulders.  
**Orange:** Strong directional light. Casts long shadows. High contrast between lit side and shadow side.  
**Golden Sunrise:** Full illumination. Sub-surface scattering on skin. Hair catches bright warm rim light.  

**How light interacts with:**
* **Hair:** Picks up environmental colors; strong rim light on edges; never entirely black in sunlight.
* **Eyes:** Catchlights are crucial for life; reflect the primary light source (neon, horizon, sun).
* **Jacket:** Absorbs light; reflects very little except in heavy rain.
* **Skin:** Realistic anime shading; soft transitions, no harsh comic-book shadows.
* **Wet surfaces:** Increased contrast and specular highlights on clothing and skin.

## Camera Consistency

**Wide Shot:** Character occupies less than 20% of the frame. Focus on isolation and scale.  
**Medium Shot:** Waist up. Used for interaction with the immediate environment.  
**Medium Close-up:** Chest up. Used for emotional resonance and internal thought.  
**Close-up:** Face and neck. Pure emotion. Background blurred (shallow depth of field).  
**Over-the-Shoulder:** Connects the character to what they are seeing.  
**Drone Shot:** Character is a small detail in a massive urban geometry.  
**Negative Space rules:** Always leave 30-40% of the frame open for UI, lyrics, or editing movement.  
**Premiere Pro friendly framing:** Do not place crucial character details on the extreme edges where they might be cropped by a 16:9 safe-zone or zoom punch-in.  

---

## Rendering Rules

* **Anime cinematic:** Feature-film quality, high fidelity.
* **Grounded realism:** Physics, lighting, and anatomy must make sense.
* **Natural anatomy:** No enlarged heads, oversized eyes, or elongated limbs.
* **Natural clothing folds:** Fabric behaves like real cotton, denim, and canvas.
* **Natural weather:** Rain falls in streaks, clouds have volume.
* **No fantasy:** Zero magical elements.
* **No cyberpunk:** Modern day only. No neon circuitry or futuristic tech.
* **No magical particles:** Dust motes and rain only. No glowing orbs or sparkles.
* **No exaggerated expressions:** Keep it subtle. No sweat drops, vein marks, or chibi reactions.
* **No oversaturated colors:** Maintain the designated color script.
* **Maintain visual continuity:** Every scene must look like it belongs to the same film.
